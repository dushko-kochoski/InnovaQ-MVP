Template: Low stock alert

1. Low-stock alerts

Before

You find out an item ran out when a shop owner from Tetovo calls angry that his Friday delivery is short. The warehouse "knew", but the message never made it from the floor to the office.

With InnovaQ

The moment any product drops below your threshold — say, 10 boxes — purchasing gets an instant email with the item, remaining quantity, and last supplier price. You reorder days before customers ever notice.

Template: Automatic supplier order

2. Automatic supplier orders

Before

Reordering means someone remembers, calls the supplier in Greece or Serbia, dictates quantities over the phone, and hopes nothing was lost in translation. Orders go out late, in batches, when "there's finally time".

With InnovaQ

When stock hits the reorder point, InnovaQ sends the purchase order straight to your supplier's system — exact items, exact quantities, no phone call, no typos. Your buyer just confirms deliveries instead of composing them.

Template: B2B customer follow-up

3. Win-back for quiet B2B customers

Before

A mini-market in Kumanovo that ordered every two weeks goes quiet. Nobody notices for two months — your salespeople are busy with the loud customers. By the time someone calls, a competitor's truck is parked out front.

With InnovaQ

Every Monday, InnovaQ scans for customers with no order in 30+ days and emails them a personal follow-up with your current offer — and tells your sales rep who to call. Quiet customers get loud again before they're gone.

Stock that watches itself. Customers that don't slip away.
